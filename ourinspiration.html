  • “Injabulo” (from Zulu language) means “The happiness/ excitement/ other synonyms”. In a proper communication, this word is always found at the titles of the context or at the very beginning of sentences only. So as the words “The happiness” in English, you can find this format at the title and also at the very beginning of the sentence even though you can also find them anywhere in English sentences, but we have corresponded precisely our translation. “Kokujabula” (from Zulu language) means “Of the happiness/ excitement/ other synonyms”. In a proper communication, the prefix “Koku-“refers specifically to any non-living thing without spelling its exact name (it may be avoiding calling or really forgotten its word) and the suffix “Jabula” specifically refers to the translation “be happy”. For this one we are going to make a quick simple example: Imagine we are talking about the sound system with avoiding to call its name, we can say “lokhu kokujabula” to precisely translate “This thing of being happy”.
